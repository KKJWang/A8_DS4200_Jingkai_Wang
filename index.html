Context

COVID-19 is a disease caused by a novel coronavirus strain, which is known to cause respiratory infections in people. The outbreak of this new strain was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, China, where a pneumonia outbreak of unknown origin emerged.

In the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic, there has been a significant amount of discussion on social media and news outlets about the importance of getting vaccinated against the disease. With the number of COVID-19 cases increasing rapidly, this dataset provides Twitter tweets containing the hashtag #CovidVaccine.

This data set can be utilized to delve into the topics that utilize the hashtag, analyze the geographical distribution, assess sentiments, and examine trends.

Data encodings

Column Name Data Encoding
user_name The name of the user, as they've defined it
user_location The user-defined location for this account's profile
user_description The user-defined UTF-8 string describing their account
user_created Time and date, when the account was created
user_followers The number of followers an account currently has
user_friends The number of friends an account currently has
user_favourites The number of favorites a account currently has
user_verified When true, indicates that the user has a verified account
date UTC time and date when the Tweet was created
text The actual UTF-8 text of the Tweet
hashtags All the other hashtags posted in the tweet along with #CovidVaccine
source Utility used to post the Tweet, for example, web
is_retweet Indicates whether this Tweet has been Retweeted

Observation and Insights

- Top locations of the tweets

  1. 1. India: 8.70%
  2. 2. United States: 7.43%
  3. 3. England: 5.96%

India has the highest percentage of tweets about Covid-19, which may be due to the severity of the outbreak in the country. India has been one of the hardest-hit countries by the pandemic, with high case numbers and a struggling healthcare system, which could be driving conversations and concerns on social media.

The United States, despite having a lower percentage of tweets than India, still has a significant presence on social media regarding Covid-19. This could be due to a number of factors, including the size of the country, the impact of the pandemic on the economy and daily life, and the political climate around Covid-19.

England, with the third-highest percentage of Covid-19 tweets, may be reflective of the situation in the UK, which has seen several waves of the pandemic and significant public health measures such as lockdowns and vaccine rollouts.

Overall, the high percentage of tweets about Covid-19 in these locations suggests that the pandemic continues to be a major concern and topic of discussion around the world, with people using social media to share information, express their opinions, and seek support or guidance.

- Top source of the tweets

  1. 1. Twitter Web App
  2. 2. Twitter for iPhone
  3. 3. Twitter for Android

The Twitter Web App is the top source of tweets about Covid-19, which suggests that many people are accessing and engaging with Covid-related content on Twitter from their desktop or laptop computers. This may reflect the fact that people are spending more time at home during the pandemic, and therefore are more likely to use their computers to access social media.

Twitter for iPhone is the second most popular source of tweets about Covid-19, indicating that many people are using their mobile devices to engage with Covid-related content on Twitter. This may reflect the fact that people are increasingly relying on their smartphones for news and information, as well as for staying connected with others during the pandemic.

Twitter for Android is the third most popular source of tweets about Covid-19, which suggests that Android smartphone users are also engaging with Covid-related content on Twitter. This may reflect the fact that Android is a popular mobile operating system used by many people around the world.

Overall, the fact that these three sources make up the majority of Covid-related tweets suggests that Twitter is a popular platform for people to share and consume information about the pandemic, regardless of the device they are using. It also suggests that people are actively using social media to stay informed and engaged with the ongoing situation, as well as to share their thoughts and experiences with others.

- Top hashtags

  1. 1. Covid vaccine: 290,000
  2. 2. Covid19: 70,000
  3. 3. Covid: 20,000

The hashtag "#Covid vaccine" is by far the most popular hashtag used in Covid-related tweets, with 290,000 uses. This suggests that people are highly interested in the development, rollout, and efficacy of Covid vaccines. It also indicates that people are using Twitter to share information and news about vaccines, as well as to express their opinions and concerns about them.

The hashtag "#Covid19" is the second most popular hashtag, with 70,000 uses. This is a general hashtag that encompasses all aspects of the Covid-19 pandemic, including news, updates, personal experiences, and more. Its popularity indicates that people are using Twitter to stay informed about the ongoing situation, as well as to share their own thoughts and experiences related to Covid-19.

The hashtag "#Covid" is the third most popular, with 20,000 uses. This is another general hashtag that can be used to encompass any aspect of the pandemic. Its popularity suggests that people are using Twitter to discuss various topics related to Covid-19, including news updates, personal experiences, and opinions.

Overall, the popularity of these hashtags suggests that Twitter is an important platform for people to discuss and engage with the ongoing Covid-19 pandemic. It indicates that people are using social media to stay informed about the situation, share information and news, express their opinions and concerns, and connect with others during a time of uncertainty and upheaval.
